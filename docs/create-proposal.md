# Rule: Creating a Client Proposal

## The Non-Negotiable Pricing Rule

**Every proposal MUST be built around value, not hours.**

Before writing a single line of a proposal, you must answer two questions:

1. **How much does this client make per month or per year?**
2. **How much money will we save them — or help them make more of?**

If you cannot answer both questions, you do not have enough information to write the proposal. Ask the client or research it first.

The price we charge must be anchored to their revenue and the value we deliver — not to the hours we spend. A client doing $3M/year in revenue should not be paying the same as a startup doing $50K/year, even if the work takes the same time.

---

## Why This Rule Exists

We learned this the hard way. A wine business doing $200-400K/month hired us to build a data platform that would save 20+ hours/month of manual work and give them real margin visibility across $3M+ in annual revenue. We charged $2,800. The value was worth $20,000+.

Pricing based on hours is a race to the bottom. Pricing based on value delivered is how you build a real business.

---

## Proposal Structure

Every proposal must include:

### 1. Client Business Context
- What does this client do?
- Estimated monthly/annual revenue
- How many employees / how big is the operation?

### 2. The Problem (in money terms)
- What is the pain costing them? (time × hourly rate, lost revenue, missed decisions)
- Example: "20 hours/month of manual reconciliation × $150/hr = $3,000/month wasted"
- Example: "No margin visibility = pricing decisions made blind on $3M revenue"

### 3. What We're Building
- Clear deliverables, not vague descriptions
- Milestones with definitions of done

### 4. The Value We Deliver
- Money saved per month/year
- Revenue protected or unlocked
- Time freed up
- Risk reduced

### 5. Our Price
- Anchored to value, not hours
- Rule of thumb: charge 10-20% of the annual value delivered
- Example: saves $3,000/month = $36,000/year → charge $5,000-7,000, not $2,800

### 6. Why Us + Why Now
- Speed of delivery
- Domain understanding
- AI-native approach

---

## Clarifying Questions Before Writing Any Proposal

Always ask or research:

1. What is the client's annual revenue (approximate)?
2. How many hours per week/month does their team spend on the problem we're solving?
3. What does a bad decision cost them? (e.g., wrong inventory = unsold stock, wrong pricing = margin loss)
4. What's the cost of NOT solving this problem over the next 12 months?

---

## Final Check Before Sending

- [ ] Do I know their approximate revenue?
- [ ] Have I calculated the value we deliver in dollar terms?
- [ ] Is our price at least 10% of the annual value we deliver?
- [ ] Is the price justified by value, not by hours?
- [ ] Would I be embarrassed to show this price to someone who knows their revenue?
